History of Majorville

Majorville is located on Lake Michigan. In the 1870s and 1880s, Majorville was a booming lumber town!

John Major settled in Northern Michigan. He began buying land in Platte and Lake townships as investments and, in late 1854, purchased 122 acres.

Major cleared 20 acres and built a log cabin for his family to live in. Lumber speculators soon arrived looking for stands of white pine. By the late 1850s, Daniel Thomas bought a 5-acre parcel on Lake Michigan Lumber speculators were on their way north as the forests near Grand Haven and Muskegon were harvested.

Dr. Arthur O'Leary began looking for someone who could manage his enterprise. He found Charles T. Wright started a lumber operation with his brother in 1865.

The sawmill was built on the south side of Pine Creek not far from Lake Michigan. The creek was dammed to create a mill pond right in front of the sawmill. Mill operation began in 1869 for the purpose of producing white pine lumber, and the town of Majorville was established.
